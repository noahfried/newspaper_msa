Bibliography
United States Newspaper Panel, 1869-2004

This bibliography includes published and unpublished works that are related 
to ICPSR study 30261. It includes works that are based on primary
or secondary analysis of the data, or which describe or critique those
data or the collection methodology. 

If you publish a work or know of other works that are based on these data,
please send the complete citation and name of the ICPSR study used to:
icpsr-biblio-notify@umich.edu

disease reporting during the health transition." Economica. Jul 2017, 84,
(335), 393 - 416. DOI: 10.1111/ecca.12227

2. Fortunato, David; Stevenson, Randolph T., "Party government and
political information." Legislative Studies Quarterly. 2021, 46, (2), 251 -
295. DOI: 10.1111/lsq.12285

3. Gentzkow, Matthew; Shapiro, Jesse M.; Sinkinson, Michael, "Competition
and ideological diversity: Historical evidence from US newspapers."
American Economic Review. 2014, 104, (10), 3073 - 3114. DOI:
10.1257/aer.104.10.3073

4. Gentzkow, Matthew; Shapiro, Jesse M.; Sinkinson, Michael, "The effect of
newspaper entry and exit on electoral politics." American Economic Review.
Dec 2011, 101, (7), 2980 - 3018. DOI: 10.1257/aer.101.7.2980

5. Hennessey, Jessica, "Loss of control: Legislature changes and the state-
local relationship." Constitutional Political Economy. Dec 2014, 25, (4),
407 - 433. DOI: 10.1007/s10602-014-9174-y

6. Park, Kyeonggook Francis; Seamans, Robert; Zhu, Feng, "Homing and
platform responses to entry: Historical evidence from the U.S. newspaper

10.1002/smj.3241

7. Park, Kyeonggook; Seamans, Robert; Zhu, Feng, "Multi-Homing and Platform

DOI: 10.2139/ssrn.3048348
Abstract: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3048348

8. Schuster, Steven Sprick, "The Persuasive Power of the Fourth Estate:

Abstract: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3048348

9. Schuster, Steven Sprick, "Three Essays in Political Economy."

Abstract: https://ssrn.com/abstract=3048348
